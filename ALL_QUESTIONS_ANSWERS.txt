Why Do Anything:
1. Question: What’s driving the urgency to solve this now?
   Answer: Calculating payroll manually is taking too long and caused multiple salary errors last month. Employees are unhappy, and we need to fix this system immediately.

2. Question: How is this issue impacting the business today?
   Answer: Our HR team spends twenty hours every week tracking employee hours on paper. This causes late payments and keeps them from doing other important work.

3. Question: What happens if this problem is not addressed?
   Answer: We will continue to face compliance risks due to incorrect tax calculations. Also, frustrated employees might leave if their pay remains inaccurate and delayed.

Business Value:
4. Question: If this were solved, what measurable business improvement would you expect?
   Answer: We expect to reduce payroll processing time by ninety percent and completely eliminate calculation mistakes. This will save our HR team significant hours every week.

5. Question: How is this impacting revenue, productivity, time, or cost today?
   Answer: We lose about five thousand dollars monthly in staff hours spent fixing errors. Productivity is low because managers manually verify every single employee timesheet.

6. Question: What key business metrics are you hoping to improve?
   Answer: We want to reduce the time spent on payroll from four days to just one hour. We also aim for one hundred percent accuracy in payments.

7. Question: What business outcomes matter most to the executive sponsor?
   Answer: The sponsor wants to lower admin costs and ensure local tax compliance. They want a smooth system that keeps employee satisfaction high and reports accurate.

Why Do Anything:
1. Question: What’s driving the urgency to solve this now?
2. Question: How is this issue impacting the business today?
3. Question: What happens if this problem is not addressed?

Business Value:
4. Question: If this were solved, what measurable business improvement would you expect?
5. Question: How is this impacting revenue, productivity, time, or cost today?
6. Question: What key business metrics are you hoping to improve?
7. Question: What business outcomes matter most to the executive sponsor?

Economic Buyer:
8. Question: Who ultimately approves initiatives like this?

Champion:
9. Question: Who is most motivated to solve this problem?
10. Question: Who would champion this internally?

Decision Criteria:
11. Question: What business or technical requirements are most important in selecting a solution?

Decision Process:
12. Question: If we mapped out the path to a final decision, what steps would need to happen and who would be involved?

Paper Process:
13. Question: What internal procurement, legal, or security steps should we plan for?
14. Question: What typically slows down or delays the purchasing process?

Current Environment:

15. Question: What solutions or processes are in place today?
16. Question: What other solutions are being considered?
17. Question: What concerns or risks do you see with moving forward?

Next Steps:

18. Question: What would make sense as a next step from here?
19. Question: What additional information would be helpful for your team?
20. Question: Who should be involved in the next conversation?
